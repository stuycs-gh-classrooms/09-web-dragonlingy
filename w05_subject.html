Taylor Swift is considered to be one of the most influential artists in the world. So far, she has released 10 albums, dubbing each one a specific era (e.g. the Red era, the Speak Now era, etc.). Before the pandemic, she usually had a tour focused on the newest album released to celebrate them (e.g. the Reputation Stadium Tour, the 1989 World Tour, etc.). However, after she started the Lover tour, the brunt of the pandemic hit, and she had to cancel it. Since then, she has released three new albums (folklore, evermore, Midnights) and two new re-records (Fearless (Taylor's Version) and Red (Taylor's Version)). Now, since the pandemic is considered less severe, she has decided to go on tour again, but instead of dedicating it to one album, she has decided to dedicate it to all ten of her eras, dubbing it the ERAS TOUR. The Eras Tour started on March 17, 2023, in Glendale, Arizona and continued until August 26, Mexico City, where she would take a break until November 9 in Buenos Aires, Argentina, then another break at November 26 until February 7th, where she would play in Tokyo, Japan and continue flying around the world until November 23, Toronto, Canada, where the Eras Tour is scheduled to conclude (for now).

Surprise Songs

To spice up the tour and celebrate some of her songs that she doesn’t play live often, Swift has decided to play two different songs of her choosing (not from the setlist) each night of her tour. These songs, officially the “Acoustic Set” and informally dubbed “Surprise Songs” by the swifties (Swift’s fans), are between the 1989 (Taylor’s Version) era and the Midnights era. The first one is typically played and sung by Swift on the guitar, while the second one is on the piano. These songs could range anywhere from her first album (Taylor Swift), to her latest (Midnights), to songs written for movies (Eyes Open, Safe & Sound, Carolina, Crazier, Sweeter than Fiction, I Don’t Wanna Live Forever, etc.), to mashups (Is It Over Now? x Out Of The Woods, etc.).
Usually, once a surprise song is played, she doesn’t play it again during the tour (unless she messes up during the song). However, she has repeated some songs she hasn’t messed up, such as You’re On Your Own, Kid (Midnights), Maroon (Midnights), and Our Song (Taylor Swift). Also, during the last leg of the 2023 tour, she announced that she would be resetting the surprise song list for the 2024 tour, making all already-played songs available again.

Eras Tour: The Movie

Swift has also released a movie of the three hour long concert, titled: Taylor Swift: The Eras Tour. First released on October 13 in theaters in the U.S. at $19.89 for adults and $13.13 for children and seniors, the 2 hr 45 min movie contained most of the songs from the concert. However, Swift cut out a few sorely-missed songs, which were:

  • The Archer (Lover)
  • 'tis the d-mn season (evermore)
  • Long Live (Speak Now (Taylor's Version)) [song played during credits]
  • cardigan (folklore)
  • Wildest Dreams (1989 (Taylor's Version))

The entirety of the movie was recorded in Los Angeles, with the two surprise songs included being You're On Your Own, Kid (Midnights) and Our Song (Taylor Swift). The 2 hour 45 minute long movie is actually 30 minutes shorter than the actual concert, so not only were some songs cut out, costume changes (time spent backstage) and applause were also cut.
The movie broke box office records, scoring an earning of $261.6 million dollars, becoming the highest-grossing concert film of all time. It also amassed a record $37 million in pre-sales in the U.S. and over $100 million in pre-sales globally.

Following the explosive success of the movie in theaters, Swift would announce that the movie would be avaiable for rent on December 13 (her birthday). The price was $19.89 USD for 48 hours; a "buy" option was unavailable. This movie, called Taylor Swift: The Eras Tour [Extended Version], contains all the songs cut out of the original movie (except cardigan) as well as the original movie setlist. However, much to the chagrin of some swifties, these songs were not edited into their corresponding eras, but instead placed at the end as "EXTRA PERFORMANCES".
On February 7, 2024, Swift would announce that a new rendition of her movie (perhaps her final rendition of her movie), Taylor Swift: The Eras Tour (Taylor's Version), would be available for streaming on Disney+ on March 15, 2024. This rendition will contain the final cut song, cardigan, as well as four additional acoustic songs.

Notes

  • Unfortunately, there is no set era for Swift's debut album, Taylor Swift.
  • Before March 31, 2023, invisible string (folklore) was the first song played in the folklore era. However, after March 31, it was replaced by the 1 (folklore). This is rumored to be because of her breakup with Joe Alwyn, as invisible string is a love song while the 1 is a breakup song.
  • After July 7, her release of the Speak Now (Taylor's Version) album, the song Long Live (Speak Now (Taylor's Version)) was added to the setlist, joining Enchanted and increasing the Speak Now (Taylor's Version) era's song count to two.
  • On July 22, Swift played with the HAIM sisters, so her collab with them, no body, no crime (feat. the HAIM sisters) (evermore) replaced 'tis the d-mn season (evermore) until August 24 as the first song played in the evermore era.
  • For all the shows where Phoebe Bridgers performed as a supporting act, Swift would play her collab with her, Nothing New (feat. Phoebe Bridgers) (From The Vault) (Red (Taylor's Version)), during the Red (Taylor's Version) era.
  • On May 22 & 23, Ice Spice and Swift performed the version of Karma (Midnights) featuring Ice Spice
  • Swift announced both her re-records released in 2023 (Speak Now (Taylor's Version) and 1989 (Taylor's Version)) during the Eras Tour acoustic set
  • Swift has a new album, called The Tortured Poets Department, coming out April 19, 2024, and many fans are speculating if there will be a new era added to the tour.
